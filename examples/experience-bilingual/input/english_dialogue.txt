User: How should I do these exercises specifically? What should I pay attention to when doing squats?

Assistant: Squats are one of the best exercises for legs and core. The correct form: feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out, back straight, sit back like you're sitting in a chair, squat until thighs are parallel to the ground, then use your glute strength to stand up. Note: don't let your knees cave in, keep your weight on your heels, don't rise onto your toes.

User: Got it. I can't do standard push-ups right now, are there alternatives?

Assistant: Of course! You can start with wall push-ups: stand facing a wall, hands on wall about one step away, body in a straight line, do push-up motion. When you can do 15-20 reps, you can transition to knee push-ups (knees on the ground), and finally standard push-ups. Progressive training is important, don't rush.

User: Understood. What should I pay attention to in terms of diet?

Assistant: Exercise results are 30% training, 70% diet. Suggestions: 1. Supplement protein and carbohydrates within 30 minutes after exercise (like eggs with banana); 2. Eat more lean meats, fish, eggs, beans and other high-quality proteins; 3. Reduce refined sugars and processed foods; 4. Ensure adequate water intake, 2-3 liters per day; 5. Increase calorie intake appropriately on exercise days, control calories on rest days.

Assistant: Pack light! You'll thank yourself when hauling luggage between cities. Essentials: comfortable walking shoes (break them in before your trip), versatile clothing that can be layered, a compact first-aid kit, copies of important documents, a universal power adapter, a portable charger, and a small daypack for daily excursions. Rolling clothes saves space and reduces wrinkles. Leave expensive jewelry and gadgets at home.

User: How should I handle money and payments abroad?

Assistant: Mix of strategies works best: Notify your bank of your travel plans to avoid blocked cards. Carry a small amount of local currency for immediate needs. Use credit cards with no foreign transaction fees for larger purchases. Consider a money belt for extra security. Keep money and cards in multiple places - if one wallet is lost or stolen, you're not stranded. Also, have a backup plan like a second credit card or emergency cash hidden in your luggage.

User: What about accommodations? Should I book everything in advance or be flexible?

Assistant: A mix is ideal. Book your first few nights in each major city in advance, especially in popular tourist destinations. This gives you time to recover from jet lag and get oriented. After that, you can be more flexible. Read reviews carefully on multiple sites. Consider location carefully - being near public transportation can save time and money. Hostels are great for solo travelers - you'll meet people and get local tips from staff.

Assistant: Homesickness is completely normal, even for experienced travelers.
